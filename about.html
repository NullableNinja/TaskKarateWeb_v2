Nice People Who Train Hard

Task Karate is a friendly, focused dojo in La Crosse, WI. We build confident movers and helpful training partners through clean mechanics, practical drills, and a community-first mindset.

Meet Randy Thomson

Randy “Mr. T” Thomson has been training in the martial arts for over 30 years and teaching for more than 20 years. A U.S. Army Veteran and Fourth Degree Black Belt, Randy leads Task Karate with a simple recipe: fundamentals, respect, and lots of reps.

His style of martial arts is American Karate, based on Tae Kwon Do, and he has also trained in Judo, Aikido, Filipino Eskrima, Japanese Karate-Do, Wrestling, Boxing, Okinawan Kobudo, and Kenpo. Whether it’s demonstrating clean mechanics, correcting technique, or celebrating student progress, Randy keeps the focus on growth—one step at a time.

Ranks & Credentials

  • 5th Degree Black Belt in American Karate
  • IS3 Eskrima Kadua Instructor
  • Nidan (2nd Degree) in Japanese Karate-Do
  • Nidan (2nd Degree) in Okinawan Kobudo

Outside the dojo, Randy enjoys canoeing, fishing, guitar, and hiking. He is also a proud father of two sons, Trevin and Mason.

What We Teach

Karate

Stances, strikes, blocks, and partner drills that carry into real life—focus, balance, and composure under pressure.

IS3 • Eskrima

Inayan System III stick work to sharpen timing and distance. Structured patterns first, controlled contact once ready.

Weapons Skills

Traditional implements to refine coordination and control. Safe handling and clean mechanics come first.

How We Train

Friendly partners, clear coaching, and steady progress. We keep egos outside and growth inside.
